## 1. TL;DR — the decision

> **Decided (30 Jun 2026): one price, one tier — €495 one-time — pushed as the default offer,
> with an optional care plan and an optional monthly-subscription alternative behind it.**
> §2–§9 below document the reasoning *journey* (including the €999 exploration the decision moved
> away from) plus the market map and sourced claims that still stand. Read §1 as the decision;
> read the rest as the evidence and the why.

The earlier exploration framed €999 as "underpriced vs an agency" — true on the *value* axis, but
the *average* buyer is a solo/ZZP salon (~€33k revenue) who **won't pay €1k for a website on a
cold approach**, and the salon market visibly clears at **€375–795 one-time or €20–79/mo**.
Affordability + conversion beat value-capture while the funnel is unproven, and a two-person side
project wants simplicity + bulk cash, not a tier ladder or a recurring book to service. So:

1. **Lead with €495 eenmalig — one price, one tier, alles inbegrepen.** ~1,5% of a solo salon's
   revenue / ~1 week of profit — the side of the line they'll actually cross. It sits a notch above
   the €375–399 template farms (so it reads *custom, not template*) and is 5–10× below an agency.
   Near-zero marginal cost (mockup ~€0.05) means €495 is essentially all contribution — you're
   pricing to *what converts*, not discounting into a loss.
2. **Optional add-on — wijzigingen / onderhoud (the care plan you already had):** ongoing edits +
   hosting, **~€10–15/mo or €149/jr**, optional, *not* a support retainer. ~88% cash margin (only
   hard cost = the .nl renewal ~€16,50/jr). Cap included edits (≈2/jr), bill extra hourly.
3. **Alternative payment model — €20–79/mo subscription instead of the €495** for owners who balk
   at any upfront sum (the model PlazaXL/StudioLee prove converts cash-thin salons). **Offered,
   not pushed:** for now you prefer one-time for bulk cash and to avoid servicing recurring/churn
   as a two-person side project. Set the exact monthly point only when you need it.

**Why one price + one-time (not tiers, not recurring):** one tier is less build/quote/maintenance
work, and the tier-lift is modest anyway — a higher "Pro" tier triggers only the weaker ~50–60%
*compromise* effect, not the famous *decoy* effect (which needs a dominated, same-price option).
One-time gives the two founders bulk cash now with no churn/support treadmill, and keeps the
"eenmalig, van jou, geen verrassingen" brand intact.

**Revisit upward only on proof.** €495 deliberately leaves money on the table vs the value
delivered, to win the first customers + testimonials. If the funnel demonstrably fills — or
*overflows* — at €495, that's the signal to test the one-time higher (€595–795); until then €495
holds. See §6–§7 for what to measure.

## 2. The economic logic (why price is set by capacity, not cost)

This is the spine; everything in §4–§5 falls out of it.

- **Marginal cost ≈ €0.05.** The mockup is LLM-generated. Cost-plus pricing is therefore the
  *wrong frame* — marginal cost sets only the floor, which is irrelevant here. Price to **value**
  and to **€-per-operator-hour**.
- **Two hard constraints bind, and neither is cost:** (a) **operator hours** — solo,
  evenings/weekends → ~1–2 builds/week → a ceiling of **~50–100 builds/year**; and (b)
  **cold-outreach conversion**.
- **The cap is throughput, not the customer base.** There is **no** hard lifetime-customer
  ceiling — but solo evenings/weekends caps you at **~50–100 builds/year**, and you can't relieve
  that by spending the near-zero COGS. So this is *not* a scalable-SaaS business where volume is
  the growth lever; growth comes from **margin per build**. Whenever demand exceeds your slots,
  every euro under-priced is lost across a constrained number of builds — so the objective is
  **max €-per-operator-hour / €-per-build, not units sold.**
- **Implied operator rate today:** €999 ÷ ~4–12 finishing/revision/handover hours per build =
  **~€83–250/operator-hour**, already at or above the NL senior freelance designer rate
  (~€72–75 avg, €120–160 senior). *Price is not the binding constraint — there is headroom.*
  (The hour count is an estimate, not a measured value — see open questions §7.)

**The conclusion the math forces:** with COGS ≈ 0 and capped *throughput*, you should push price
up to the point where conversion starts costing you a slot you could otherwise have filled. The
only reason *not* to raise today is that you don't yet know where that point is — the funnel is unproven.

## 3. Where €999 actually sits in the NL market

The NL "website laten maken" market is sharply laddered. €999 (VAT-incl., ≈ €826 ex-btw) lands
**at the top of the template/productized band and at the entry/floor of the custom band:**

| Band | Typical NL price | Model | Where €999 sits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Salon "website-farms"** (Areza, PlazaXL, 1DagOnline, StudioLee) | €250–399 one-time **or** €25–79/mo | template; setup+monthly or pure subscription | €999 is **above** them — must read as *custom, not template* |
| **Productized one-time tiers** (zzpwebsitemaken kapper) | €495 / €795 / €1.295 ex-btw (+ €39–69/mo optional) | template, one-time | €999 brackets between Compleet (€795) and Premium (€1.295) |
| **Closest custom comp — Alydo** | **"vanaf €1.000"**, custom kapper site, **4–6 weken** | custom, one-time | €999 **matches its floor** and beats it ~5× on speed (5 dagen) |
| **Freelancer / agency custom (maatwerk)** | €2.500–5.000+ (agency to €7.500–15.000) | custom, one-time + hosting | €999 is **2–5× below** a comparable custom quote — the wedge |
| **DIY builders** (Wix, Squarespace, JouwWeb/Webador) | €8.50–15/mo (ad-free + own domain) | self-build subscription | break-even vs €1.000 ≈ **year 6–10**; can't win on cost |

Three things this map establishes:

1. **You cannot win on cost** — a DIY tool is genuinely cheaper than €999 for ~6–10 years.
   Lead instead on **done-for-you** (the salon's scarce input is chair-time, not money),
   **custom** (rivals are templated; Alydo is custom but ~5× slower), and **ownership / Google-
   vindbaarheid** (the cheap floor is either an ads-on `jouwweb.nl` subdomain or a platform-owned
   profile that ranks *the platform*, not them).
2. **The market norm is recurring** (€20–79/mo, often mandatory). Revivo's **"eenmalig, geen
   verplicht abonnement"** is a real differentiator — and the optional €15/mo care plan undercuts
   *every* rival's mandatory plan. The honest lifetime-cost line: *a €30/mo competitor = €1.080
   over 3 years and keeps climbing; Revivo is cheaper over the lifetime with no lock-in.*
3. **€999 is "custom-quality at template-adjacent price."** That's the position the ~€0.05 mockup
   makes economically possible and no human competitor can match — but it also means €999 is
   priced like the *cheap* custom option when the *output* is agency-grade. That gap is the
   underpricing.

## 4. The recommendation in detail

### 4a. Price point & sequencing

- **Hold €999 for batch one**, fixed, as the clean baseline. Standardize on it everywhere (the
  PDF's €1.000 → €999, or vice-versa — just **pick one**; alternating reads as carelessness on a
  premium frame).
- **Yes — €999 is expensive for the *average* (solo) salon; it sits at their affordability edge.**
  3.0% of a ZZP salon's annual revenue (€33,257), ~4.6% of net profit (€21,611, *single-source —
  treat as indicative*), ≈ 2.4 weeks' profit. The 81% solo majority *is* the average buyer, so
  this is the binding affordability fact. Resistance climbs sharply above ~€1.500, where they
  revert to €495 template shops — so **do not push the floor higher for them.** The close depends
  on the mockup making the value unmistakable; €999 is defensible only *because* they've already
  seen the finished site.
- **The staffed/multi-location salon is the exception** (the ~19% with personeel): €999 is **0.4%**
  of their revenue (€247,197) — they don't feel it, and value brand/ownership more. This is where
  the genuine underpricing lives. Capture it with a *higher tier/upsell*, **not** by lifting the
  floor on the solo majority who can't comfortably absorb it.
- **A solo-floor raise to ~€1.250 is a *test*, not a default.** €999 is already near the average
  salon's ceiling; €1.250 = ~3.8% of revenue / ~6 weeks' profit — into flinch territory. Test it
  only if the funnel is clearly *overflowing* at €999 (you'd then be selecting the most willing
  buyers, who skew toward better-capitalised salons anyway). Otherwise hold the solo floor at €999
  and take the upside from the staffed upsell + the care plan.

### 4b. Model: one-time spine + optional passive care plan

**Keep the model one-time. Do not make maintenance mandatory.** The one-time core (i) fits the
ownership / no-lock-in positioning, (ii) closes faster on a thin cold funnel, and — decisively —
(iii) avoids the open-ended retention/support liability a subscription imposes. SMB-focused SaaS
churns **3–7%/mo (~31–58%/yr)**; a mandatory plan would stack a growing retention treadmill on
top of your *fixed* build capacity, which is exactly the constraint you can't relieve. (Caveat:
that benchmark is software-SaaS, not website-care, which is stickier — but the direction holds.)

### 4c. The care plan (the only LTV lever)

- **Keep optional, reframe as passive:** hosting + SSL + domain + **a capped ~2 small content
  edits/year**, **€15/mo or €149/jr vooruitbetaald**. Explicitly **not** a support retainer.
- **The margin is real and grounded:** the only hard recurring cash cost is the **.nl renewal
  (~€16,50/jr, TransIP — and €0 if the customer owns the domain)**; SSL is free (Let's Encrypt);
  and hosting is ~€0 marginal — **Vercel Pro is billed per *seat*, not per project** (one seat
  hosts every customer site → ~€0,40/site/mo at 50 customers) and **Netlify's Free tier explicitly
  permits commercial use.** So €180/jr revenue carries ~€21/jr hard cost = **~88% cash gross
  margin**; charge ~1 operator-hour for the 2 edits and it's still **~60% contribution margin.**
- **Positioning:** €15/mo sits **below every mandatory competitor** (PlazaXL €19,95, zzpwebsitemaken
  €39–69, StudioLee €79, Antum €69) and only just above the cheapest *hosting-excluded* WordPress
  floor (€13,50) — so being **cheaper *and* optional** than a rival's *mandatory* plan is the wedge,
  and it never reignites a "duur abonnement" objection.
- **A legit alternative:** €19,95/mo = exact **PlazaXL parity** (still ~90% margin) anchors the
  maintenance value higher. But the care plan is a *relationship/goodwill* lever, not the profit
  centre, so I'd keep the **€15/jr-or-€149 "fair, optional, no-lock-in"** framing as the deliberate
  contrast to the mandatory crowd. Either is defensible; this is a minor knob.
- **The one structural rule** (more important than the price): the binding risk is **edit labour**,
  not infra — so **cap included edits (2/jr) and bill extra changes hourly.** The moment the plan
  drifts into open-ended support it taxes the build capacity that caps the whole business. Annual
  prepay (€149/jr) also kills churn-admin.
- **MRR is a compounding installed-base annuity** on top of each one-time: at a 50% attach rate
  that's ~€90/jr of near-pure margin per customer you've *ever* sold to — it accumulates as the
  base grows (e.g. ~€750/mo across 100 attached customers), for near-zero marginal effort.
- Present it **default-checked at handover** as the convenient option — preserving the "geen
  verplicht abonnement" headline against the subscription crowd.

## 5. The genuine fork — publish tiers, or keep one price?

The textbook revenue-capture move is a **3-tier ladder** (e.g. Basis €999 / Studio €1.450 /
Pro €2.450): a high anchor lifts the average and segments the ZZP buyer from the staffed buyer
without discounting. **But it directly undercuts the brand's core promise** — the proposal's
entire pitch is *"Eenmalig. Geen verrassingen. Een vaste prijs voor alles. Alles inbegrepen."*
Three prices in a cold WhatsApp/IG opener also add decision friction the OUTREACH playbook works
hard to remove (one link, one ask, price only on the call).

**A caution on the textbook math** (from the verification pass — don't over-promise the lift):
the dramatic "decoy moves choice 32%→84%" (Ariely) is the **asymmetric-dominance** effect and
requires a *dominated, same-price* option — a higher Pro tier is **not** that. A higher anchor
invokes the **weaker compromise effect**: realistically **~50–60% pick the middle**, not the
often-quoted 66%. So tiers help, but modestly.

**My recommendation: keep ONE published price + a discreet, quote-based upsell.**

- **Published, everywhere (opener, marketing, PDF):** the single simple price (€999 now →
  ~€1.250 later). Protects the "geen verrassingen" promise and the 81%-ZZP-majority close.
- **On the call only, when the salon is clearly staffed / multi-location:** a
  *"meerdere vestigingen / extra pagina's"* option at **~€1.950–2.450**, presented as a natural
  scope-up, not a published tier. This captures the segment where €999 is 0.4% of revenue without
  diluting the simple offer for everyone else, and it costs you nothing if it's never taken.

If you'd rather maximize captured value over brand-simplicity, the 3-published-tier version is the
alternative — it's a legitimate choice, just a different bet. **This is the one decision in this
doc that's a judgment call for you, not a number the research settles.**

## 6. What NOT to do

- **Don't raise the floor before the funnel is proven.** A higher price on an unfilled funnel
  starves capacity *and* costs you a clean baseline. Sequence: prove fill at €999 → then raise.
- **Don't discount reflexively.** A fast drop tells a Dutch buyer the €999 was fake (OUTREACH §4).
  Prefer an honest payment split or a *true* intro price.
- **Don't drift below ~€900.** That collapses the "custom, not template" perception the whole
  positioning rests on and drops you into the €495–795 template-farm band.
- **Don't make maintenance mandatory**, and don't let the care plan become support-heavy.
- **Don't sell the price as "marketing spend"** — real salon marketing budgets are tiny (~2% of
  revenue ≈ €630/yr), so €999 reads huge there. Frame it as a **multi-year capital asset**
  (~€200–330/yr amortized) that you *own*.
- **Don't anchor against a Treatwell-commission saving** — there is none (the site embeds
  Treatwell). Anchor against agency quotes and perpetual DIY/competitor subscriptions.

## 7. Open questions — what to measure before raising price

1. **Can outreach already fill 1–2 builds/week at €999?** *The* number. It decides whether to
   raise the floor or hold. Raise only until the funnel *just* fills capacity.
2. **What close/reply lift does the personalized mockup buy vs a text-only opener?** Quantifies
   how much pricing headroom the moat actually creates.
3. **What share of sourced leads are staffed/multi-location vs solo ZZP?** Sizes the Pro upsell
   and whether a dedicated multi-location outreach lane is worth it. (NB: OUTREACH §9.11 warns
   bigger salons more often *already have a site* — addressable Pro volume may be thin.)
4. **Care-plan attach rate + true support hours/customer/year?** Validates the €15/mo passive
   framing and whether the MRR is worth the (small) ongoing obligation.
5. **Real operator-hours per build?** Turns the €83–250/hr estimate into a measured number — the
   true denominator for the €-per-operator-hour objective.
